Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the practice of using technology and the internet safely, responsibly, respectfully, and ethically.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the rules and laws that govern the use of technology and the internet. It includes laws about copyright, plagiarism, privacy, security, and acceptable online behavior to ensure people use digital content legally and responsibly.

What is AUP?

AUP (Acceptable Use Policy) is a set of rules that explains how users are allowed to use a school's, company's, or organization's network, internet, and digital resources. I

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's words, ideas, or work as your own without giving proper credit to the original source.

It is considered dishonest and can have serious academic or legal consequences.

Academic Deshonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair advantage or misrepresents their learning, such as cheating, plagiarism, or having someone else complete an assignment.

It violates academic rules and undermines learning and integrity.

What are the rules for AI Generated content?

The rules for AI-generated content require students to follow instructor guidelines and not submit AI work as their own unless permitted.
