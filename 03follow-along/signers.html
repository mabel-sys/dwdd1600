Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson was a prominent leader from Virginia who helped shape American ideals of liberty and democracy.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence from Massachusetts, Adams played a major role in the Continental Congress and later served as the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Representing Pennsylvania, Franklin was a renowned statesman, inventor, and diplomat who helped secure support for the American cause, especially in France.
John Hancock
As the President of the Second Continental Congress, John Hancockâ€™s bold and prominent signature is one of the most recognizable on the Declaration of Independence.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams and an influential revolutionary leader from Massachusetts, Samuel Adams was instrumental in organizing resistance to British policies.
Richard Henry Lee
A Virginian who proposed the resolution for independence, his motion set the stage for drafting the Declaration of Independence.
Roger Sherman
Representing Connecticut, Sherman was the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States: the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, the Constitution, and the Articles of Association.
Robert Morris
A financier from Pennsylvania who played a critical role in funding the Revolutionary War, Morris later signed the Constitution as well.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and are remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration of Independence. By placing their names on the document, they openly challenged British rule and risked their lives, fortunes, and reputations, as the act was considered treason by the British Crown. Their commitment helped lay the foundation for a new nation built on the principles of liberty and self-government.
